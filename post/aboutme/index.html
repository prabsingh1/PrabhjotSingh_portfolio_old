About Me

What’s this portfolio for?

I am a current Masters in Data Science student at the University of Virginia. I enjoy using data to explore a vareity of different things that I care about including weather, sports and healthcare. This portfolio is to describe my data science journey and highlights some of the projects I have completed so far. If you have any questions feel free to contact me on any of my socials!

Undergrad

I attended the University of Virginia (UVa) for my undergrad. After taking a introductory child psychology class, I was intrigued into joining the Early Social Development Lab at UVa as a Research Assistant. At the lab my primary role was to carry out studies with children as young as a couple months old. One of my favorite studies dealt with morality. and I also leanred a bit about SPSS and how it is used in the research world to code and analyze data. Overall I throughouly enjoyed my experience at the lab and was integral in influencing me to major in Psychology.

Alongside this, I was very interested in going to medical school. My mom was a nurse and would always tell me about how much of a privledge it was to be able to care for people. This instilled in me a passion to help people and thus take pre-medical school classes like Pysics, Organic Chemsity, and Biochemsity. I also interned in a Neuro-Oncology Clinical Trials Lab where I organized a multitude of studies and was exposed to medical research development cycle.

Gap Year - Start of Data Science

However, somewhere along my undergrad I became hesitant about signing off the next 1o years of my life becoming a doctor. The primary reason to this was myriad of problems that structurally make up medicine - leading to incredibly high rates of physican burnout and poor quality of care. Therefore I decided to intern in a healthcare non-profit company called the Get the Medications Right Instutute(GTMRx), where I learned about many of ways in which care can be improved. One of the main ways which I found the most compelling was by leveraging data.

Currently healthcare is in a lag when it comes to the use of technology. Although a vast amount of care facilities use an electronic health records(EHR) system of some sort, it is outdated and doesn’t adequately use any of the information that is taken down. However, as learned from my time at GTMRx, there are vareity of companies that are actively tyring to change this, and utilize data available in the EHRs to create powerful predictive algorithms. In doing so, the whole healthcare landscape can be changed drastically, leading to automated systems that can better monitor peoples health, and alleviating the strain that it is oftentimes placed on doctors and healthcare practicioners. There are many other promising ways in which technology may be used in medicine, including the emergence of whole genome sequening.

Overall, my time at GTMRx was instrumental in peaking my interest in data science and pushing me to persue a Masters in Data Science.
